## Module 1: Introduction to Copilot in Microsoft Edge (10 Minutes)

### What is Copilot in Edge?

Copilot is an AI-powered assistant integrated directly into the Microsoft Edge browser that helps you:
- ✅ Draft and refine written communications
- ✅ Analyze and summarize web pages and documents
- ✅ Extract insights from complex information
- ✅ Accelerate research and decision-making

### Key Features

| Feature | Description | Use Case Example |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Writing Assistant** | Helps draft emails, reports, and documents | "Draft an executive update email" |
| **Web Page Analysis** | Summarizes and extracts insights from active pages | "Summarize the key findings from this report" |
| **PDF Analysis** | Analyzes documents directly in browser | "Extract the top 3 risks from this document" |
| **Iterative Refinement** | Allows follow-up questions to drill deeper | "Expand on point 2 with more detail" |

### How to Access Copilot

**Step 1:** Open Microsoft Edge browser
**Step 2:** Click the Copilot icon in the upper right corner
**Step 3:** The Copilot sidebar opens on the right side

**✏️ Practice Now:** Open Copilot and type "Hello, what can you help me with?" in the chat.

---

### Setting Expectations: What Copilot Does Well

✅ **Copilot Excels At:**
- Drafting first versions of communications
- Summarizing long documents quickly
- Extracting specific information from content
- Reformatting information for different audiences
- Brainstorming ideas and alternatives
- Translating technical content to business language

---

### Understanding Limitations: Where Human Judgment is Essential

⚠️ **Copilot Should Not Be Used For:**
- Making final business decisions without human review
- Handling confidential or regulated data (unless policy allows)
- Generating content used verbatim without editing
- Tasks requiring real-time, legally binding information

## Module 2: Writing and Communication with Copilot (20 Minutes)

### The RIFCC Pattern for Effective Prompts

A structured approach to prompting ensures better results:

**R - Role:** Define Copilot's role (e.g., "Act as a professional communications assistant")
**I - Input:** Provide the raw material or context
**F - Format:** Specify output format (email, bullet points, executive brief)
**C - Context:** Explain the situation and audience
**C - Constraints:** Set boundaries (word limit, tone, what to avoid)

---

## Module 4: Responsible Use — Data Handling and Security Compliance (20 Minutes)

### The Critical Importance of Verification

⚠️ **Warning:** AI models can sometimes "hallucinate" (generate plausible-sounding but false information).

**Never copy-paste-send AI output without verification.**

---

### The 5-Step Review Process

Before using ANY AI-generated content, apply this review:

**1. ACCURACY:** Are the facts correct and verifiable?
**2. SOURCE:** Can I trace each claim to a reliable source?
**3. TONE:** Is the language appropriate for my audience?
**4. POLICY:** Does this comply with company data-handling guidelines?
**5. LOGIC:** Does the reasoning make sense, or are there unsupported leaps?

---

### Exercise 4.3: Apply the 5-Step Verification Process

**Step 1:** Copy the prompt below
**Step 2:** Paste into Copilot

#### Copilot Prompt (Copy and Paste)

```
Before I finalize the strategic memo for the CFO, I need you to perform a critical self-review of your previous analysis. Apply the following 5-step verification process:

1. **ACCURACY CHECK:** What specific assumptions did you make about the financial data that were not explicitly stated in the documents?

2. **SOURCE VERIFICATION:** For each major claim in your recommendation (costs, ROI, market trends), identify the exact source. If you referenced information not present in the provided documents, list it explicitly.

3. **TONE ASSESSMENT:** Is the recommendation appropriately cautious given the financial risks, or is it overly optimistic?

4. **POLICY ALIGNMENT:** Did your analysis respect the constraint that I can only use information from the two provided documents (trends report and financial data)?

5. **LOGIC CHECK:** Walk through your go/no-go recommendation step-by-step. Are there any logical gaps or unsupported leaps in reasoning?

Provide a structured response addressing each of the 5 points above.
```
